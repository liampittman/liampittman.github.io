About Me

Professional Photo

Hi, I'm William Pittman, a data journalist, visual storyteller, and investigative researcher based in south Mississippi. I can honestly say every one of those job titles is an accident. In early 2020, just before the world turned on end, I was dabbling in data analysis for fun. The chaos of COVID spurred the launch of the Mississippi Free Press and not only did those skills suddenly matter but I was positioned to use them. I found myself figuring out how to make sense of numbers that impacted real people, and I've been hooked on data-driven storytelling ever since.

It wasn't long before I was even more drawn to geography than I was data analysis. I have a habit of chasing after whatever sparks my curiosity, and literally everything has a spatial dimension. Geography gives me a way to connect those threads‚Äîwhether it's mapping how a virus spreads, tracking election changes, or visualizing where public money actually goes. To say my relationship to this place is complicated would be an understatement, but I choose to focus my energy on the hope I feel for the inhabitants of this state. I want my work to help people, Mississippians and outsiders alike, to see systems more clearly, ask better questions, and maybe make a bit of progress where it‚Äôs needed.
